Paul Crossley is a British concert pianist known particularly for his interpretations of twentieth-century repertoire.

Born in Yorkshire, he studied piano from an early age before continuing his musical education in Paris. There he worked with the composer Olivier Messiaen and the pianist Yvonne Loriod, whose influence shaped his approach to colour and sonority.

Crossley first came to wider attention in the early 1970s after performing the premiere of Michael Tippett’s Third Piano Sonata. His performances and recordings of this repertoire helped bring new audiences to contemporary British music.

Over the course of his career he has recorded widely and appeared in recital halls and festivals across the world.
