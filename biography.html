Personal Details

Date of Birth: 17 May 1944

Education: Silcoates School, Wakefield; Mansfield College, Oxford (BA 1966, MA 1970)

Private Piano Study: Fanny Waterman (Leeds), Olivier Messiaen and Yvonne Loriod (Paris)

Career Highlights

Paul Crossley is internationally recognised for his virtuosity and musical insight. His repertoire spans classical and contemporary works, with particular distinction in French music and modern compositions.

He made his debut at the Festival de Tours, France in 1968, and quickly became known for championing new music, premiering works by composers such as Michael Tippett, Henze, Takemitsu, Berio, Adams, Lindberg, Maw, and Benjamin.

In 1973, Crossley gave the world premiere of Tippett’s Third Piano Sonata, gaining immediate international recognition. He also collaborated with prominent instrumentalists, including violinist Arthur Grumiaux, and has presented acclaimed television documentaries on major 20th-century composers as well as Liszt and Ravel.

Recordings and Awards

Crossley’s discography exceeds 50 albums, covering composers from Debussy, Ravel, Faure, Franck, Poulenc, Janacek, Tippett, Stravinsky, and Takemitsu. His recordings have received numerous awards, including:

  • Diapason d’Or – Ravel and Tippett piano works
  • Prix Caecilia – Berg Chamber Concerto
  • Grand Prix du Disque de l’Académie Française – Faure Violin Sonatas
  • Deutsche Schallplatten Preis – Lutoslawski Piano Concerto
  • Grand Prix du Disque, Edison Prize, Diapason d’Or, Grammy nomination – Stravinsky piano & orchestra works
  • Gramophone Contemporary Music Award – Takemitsu’s Quotation of Dream

Honours

Honorary Fellow of Mansfield College, Oxford (1991) Commander of the British Empire (CBE) (1993)

Compositions

In recent years, Crossley has also composed piano works, including English Music in memory of Michael Tippett, performed in London and Tokyo to critical acclaim.
